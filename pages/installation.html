We All Scream For Ice Cream
Installation
2023

 

 

In Jim Jarmush' seminal film "Down By Law" three prisoners, in a momentary lapse of reason speaking in Babylonian tongues, form a bond over repeating the phrase "I scream, you scream, we all scream for ice cream!". Ice cream, its figure in advertising and screaming children running to gather around the ice cream truck produce a sense of community, all to a recognizable dinky tune. In Taiwan, this moment is reproduced in the management of waste: rather than the indiscriminate beep-beep hidden by the morning fog, Taiwanese garbage trucks ring with beautiful simple melodies, letting people know it is time to gather and dispose of their communal waste. Meanwhile, as internet platforms wage war over dominance of the access portal, we find ourselves as prisoners stuck conversing from bubbles, doomscrolling in the prison of internet noise - down by law. We all scream for ice cream! explores the ice-cream model of Taiwanese waste management and the ethereal apparition of community amidst the access portal war. As an audiovisual installation, it combines found social media footage, waste and prison material to communicate the proposed narrative.

Onomy Onome
Film
2020 - 2021

 

Onomy Onome is a ~40 min. film shot in 2020/21. Onomy Onome relays the abstracted events leading up to the rape of the main character MICHELLE. I was the main sound technician for the project, involved with the recording and post-production, as well as the composing of the score. This film was conducted under the the no thing collective to be featured on a large scale UV-Crystalized display as a holographic image.

 

 

 

 

The Squares Have Taken Over
no thing collective exhibition
Feb, 2020

 

The Squares Have Taken Over was an exhibition hosted under the no thing collective in De Vondelbunker, an autonomous cultural space in Amsterdam. The event was situated around a combined visual and sonic experience - to encapsulate the dynamics how these can develop and shape the experience of the space during the exhibition. The music program was centered around arriving at 'the end of the world'. A live soundcape was created for the initial part of the exhibition, and subsequently the DJ'ing and music choice increased in tempo and chaoticness; symbolising a loss of control.

The Score
Installation
June, 2020

 

The Score was a planned sound installation organised by a research group on the emerging field of sound studies within the humanities. The exhibition was planned for an old unused basement within the Universiteitstheater, a building owned by the University of Amsterdam. This installation aims to steer the audience towards an awareness of the immediate situationality of their affective experience. That once one sets foot in the installation, one ceases to observe and becomes active and participatory to the experience.

 

We do this by situating the sonic impulses in multiple aural spaces; by not explicitly marking all the elements that make this installation up; by simply having the tracks which play in all the speakers be of far longer duration than most audience members will likely spend their time in this installation space. The Sonic Impulses are made up of two tracks which play in speakers in separate corners of the room. While the pockets of sound, are found in boxes with speakers in them or in headphones.
